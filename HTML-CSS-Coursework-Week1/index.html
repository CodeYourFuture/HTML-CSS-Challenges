Sheikh Jarrah .. the Full Story of a Palestinian Neighborhood that Fights Judaization and Settlements

The village of Sheikh Jarrah

The history of the village and its foundation goes back to more than 900 years ago, and the village took its name from Prince Hussam al-Din bin Sharaf al-Din Issa al-Jarahi, who is the doctor of the Muslim leader Salah al-Din al-Ayyubi, and his tomb is still located in Sheikh Jarrah

The village was inhabited by well-known Palestinian families, such as the Nashashibi family. The Palestinian writer Isaaf al-Nashashibi descended from them, whose palace is still there. Sheikh Jarrah lives more than 3 thousand Palestinians on an area of ​​land estimated at about a thousand dunams, which is the last remaining land for them after the confiscation of thousands of dunams of the residents' lands, on which 3 settlements were established, known as the French Hill settlements.Read Mor Information

Allegations Of Occupation

After 3 years of occupying East Jerusalem, the Israeli parliament (Knesset) approved in 1970 a law claiming that Jews had land and real estate in Sheikh Jarrah, and claimed that they owned these land and real estate before the establishment of Israel. Since then, Israeli governments have succeeded in planting settlement outposts and replacing ultra-Orthodox “Haredi” Jews in the heart of the neighborhood with the settlement of “Shimon Hatsadeq” and the “Shepherd Hotel” settlement that was established in 2011, the headquarters of the occupation police, the Border Guard Command and the Israeli Ministry of Interior, which besieges the Palestinian presence in the neighborhood.

The Suffering of Sheikh Jarrah neighborhood

Imagine that you are sitting in your house that you love with your family, and a strange man opens the door of your house and tells you to get out of the house, this is my hous.This is exactly what is happening to the residents of Sheikh Jarrah neighborhood in occupied Jerusalem
In November 2008, the occupation authorities forcibly expelled the first Palestinian families from the neighborhood and settled in them for Jews, the al-Ghawi family, who were evicted by force of arms from their homes in the Sheikh Jarrah neighborhood in which they lived on it for more than 60 years

The area in which there are 28 homes and 500 Palestinians is at risk of eviction and forced displacement, and the settlement of settlers The Palestinians, because they have rights, did not surrender and were not terrified by the threats of the occupation, and they are still resisting in order to preserve their homes that are adjacent to the blessed Al-Aqsa Mosque, in which they lived and grew up for more than 60 years.
